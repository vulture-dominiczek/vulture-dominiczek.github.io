2 reasons you shouldn't call it a rewrite

So you have to extend some piece of code. Nothing out of the ordinary except that it is so spahetti you forgot what is your name while reading it. The choice is obvious let's gather the requirements, either from tests or BA's and rewrite it.

But it is the last thing management want's to hear about. Yeah just poke it a little bit run through it with debugger or just anything you want I don't care. But don't rewrite it.

Why would somebody ruin the rest of your week and possibly weekend and tell you that?

Uncertainity

Nobody knows how long it took for this thing to take current shape. This is because the developer is long gone. He probably run away from being taken responsible for writing it. But somehow it works and lot's of stuff is depending on it.

What is unclear is how long is it going to take you (given your current skillset and all this tasks complexity) to start from scratch and finish it. Management to do it's job needs estimates and nobody can give them that because nobody knows.

To be a good resource you should be predictable and do estimatable work.

Better than nothing

There is some chance that the knowledge codified in this monstrous thing leaked in the course of time. True it is there but cause-result relationships were not documented and are now opaque.

Business can deal without the new features, but it has to sustain what it already has got. Rewrite can have big consequnces after all, because you can implement it slightly different which will cause butterfly effect and make everything stop.

Conclusion

So these are 2 things you shouldn't use the word. Call it differently:

  • I refactored it intensely, but the logic is there
  • I slightly extended this and that
  • ...

and rewrite it anyway.
